Case study: Coffee trip app redesign

Enabling users to save their trip memories and business owners to track their target customers

The App

European Coffee Trip

Roles

UX designer
UX researcher

Timeline

2 months

Tools

Figma
Google forms
MS Excel

Reasons

Personal project

Summary

Project

European coffee trip is an app that features a database of over 3500 cafes in 39 countries and is regularly updated. It is built on coffee lovers baristas and people, who just want to have a nice cup of coffee. Users can find cafes based on their preferences and get navigation to the place.

Problem

In order to make the app more like other business discovery apps (Google maps, etc.), I tried validating an idea of adding a review feature.
Cafes can choose to pay a subscription in order to be featured in the app. However, they have no way of knowing if customers visit their cafe based on the coffee trip's suggestion.

Conclusion

In research I found out that none of the questioned users have ever left a review. More than 3/4 of the users travel more than 4 times a year. And all the users keep memories in the form of photos while traveling.

Decision

A review feature wouldn't be used by most users. A new feature will be based on keeping memories from coffee trips in photos.

Current search for specialty coffee

Simple app, yet downloaded by many.
European Coffee Trip app functions as an encyclopedia of specialty coffee cafes. The current app is mostly financed by listed cafes that can buy a subscription in order to be featured in the app.
The user can discover nearby cafes and filter them based on their liking.

Key takeaways

Users have never left a review about bad coffee service

In research a user said they’d appreciate a review feature.
However, to a “How did you react when you encountered a bad coffee service?” question, none of the users answered they had left a review or made a comment to staff.

Users can save their memories, businesses get data about target customers

The new special feature design aligns both business’ and users’ wants and needs.
One sollution gives users a personalised app full of their own memories from travels and business owners a way to track their target customers.

Final design

Discover the prototype

No annoying waiting to load

Current app doesn’t have a loading screen. A throbber appears on the screen while loading the app. In order to reduce users’ annoyance with a throbber a new loading screen in the form of a coffee cup was designed.

Building fondness for an app

New feature of adding coffee memories from trips was added to the menu. The trips are sorted according to cities and cafes. Memories are kept in the form of photos with a description similar to the Instagram feed (according to Jakob's law). A user gains a familiarity and fondness for the app, while business owners gain a way to keep track of frequency of cafe visits. This way the app also becomes a marketing tool for business owners.

Easily available button

So that the new feature is conveniently available in the current interface, a new button was added to a cafe page. The button replaces a navigation button as there are already 2 on a cafe page.
