Creative hair styling

Magda is an experienced hairdresser, whose work is practically art. She prefers clients who are not afraid to be bold and brave when it comes to color of their hair.
Her staple is unicorn hair- a mix of purple, blue and pink and sometimes even green on the hair of a customer.
She wishes to lure in new customers who’d seek her out to get Magda’s uniquely dyed hair. She’d like to have a logo, that will mirror her creative work and stand out among other hairdressers’ logos and brand identities.

Key takeaways

You’ll never get it done on the first try

Ideation is a crucial part of creating a brand identity. People have different ideas about terms. When they say they want something abstract, it may not mean what you’re thinking.
Client’s claims and needs should be validated by pictures. That way it gives an exact idea about the client’s wishes.

Motivating the client to market themselves

Magda fell in love with the final designs and was excited to use them on her social media and new business cards.
The whole design process motivated her to start marketing her business more on social media. I provided her with some marketing tips and tricks on how to create social media content so she would understand the whole design concept more.
What Magda appreciates the most is that she’ll be able to stand out from the crowd and do more of what she’s passionate about.

Back in time

A competitive research has shown that many hairdressing businesses have implemented scissors, hair combs, siluettes into their logo. Some logos look like they were made in year 2000.
An important wish of a client was to abandon this popular concept. Instead, building something original and never-seen-before.

“I’d like to see colours, creativity and boldness in my logo.”

-Magda

The main logo

Concept of the main logo features Magda’s favourite technique - freehand hair painting. The goal was to make typography, that will seem like it was painted with a paintbrush. The swirls connect the main logo with the submark logo.
Part of the brand identity design was to create business cards that would be cohesive with the identity.
